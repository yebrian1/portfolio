Bio-Inspired Pneumatic Modular Actuator for Peristaltic Transport

IEEE Robotics & Automation Letters — accepted May 2025 • Authors: Brian Ye, Zhuonan Hao, Priya Shah, Mohammad K. Jawed

A modular donut-shaped soft actuator system that uses sequential radial (compression) and axial (longitudinal) inflation to grasp and transport objects via peristaltic waves. Includes pressure-feedback synchronization and modular stacking.

Open paper (PDF) View on IEEE Xplore Repo Watch demo

Highlights

  • Modular compression + longitudinal donut modules for radial and axial actuation.
  • Pressure-feedback enables synchronized, multi-level transport of irregular objects.
  • Demonstrated stable transport of pens, handheld level, and other shapes at ~2.08 ± 0.07 mm/s.

Design & Fabrication

The module uses Ecoflex rings with internal chambers and a TPU strain-limiting casing. Compression modules inflate radially to grasp; longitudinal modules inflate axially to push the object forward. Multiple chambers mitigate asymmetric inflation.

Quick specs

  • Sensors: MPRLS pressure sensors
  • Controller: Arduino Mega with valves & pump

Results & notes

Module sequence affects speed; C–L–L–C increased throughput vs C–L–C in tests. Pressure thresholds reliably detect grasp/release events and allow cascade triggering across stacked levels.
