TripodBot — Self-Deployable CCPJ Robot

ISRR 2024 • Wenzhong Yan, Brian Ye, Mingxi Li, Jonathan B. Hopkins, Ankur Mehta

TripodBot uses Contracting-Cord Particle Jamming (CCPJ) beams to self-deploy into a tripod, tune stiffness, and perform slip-stick crawling. It demonstrates strong adaptability and load-bearing capabilities.

Open paper (PDF) View on ResearchGate Back to projects

Key contributions

  • CCPJ beams (beads + contracting cord) that self-assemble and tune stiffness via jamming.
  • TripodBot: self-deploying tripod that crawls with directional friction and shape change.
  • Performance: speed ~0.51 m/min, climbs 15°, navigates gaps down to ~31% height, and large static load-bearing (SCP actuators).

Mechanism & control

Each CCPJ beam contains beads threaded over SMA wire with initial slack. Heating the SMA straightens and increases stiffness through particle jamming. The locomotion uses grouped square-wave actuation for stick-slip motion.

Experimental highlights

  1. Beam stiffness increased >50× when current increased to 0.4 A.
  2. Self-deployment expands bounding box by ~43×.
  3. Adaptable navigation through confined tunnels and load tests including a 19.8 kg footstep (with SCP actuator variant).
Specs & materials
  • Beads: plywood 3 mm
  • Actuator: SMA wire (0.15 mm) / SCP for robustness
  • Control: simple square-wave current

Repro tips

Thread beads with designed slack, tune SMA current & actuation period, and adjust claws/leg tips for directional friction. For untethered operation consider SCP actuators and onboard power.
